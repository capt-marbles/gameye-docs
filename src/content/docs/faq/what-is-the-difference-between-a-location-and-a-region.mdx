---
title: 'What is the difference between a location and a region?'
description: "Clarifies how Gameye models regions versus locations for traffic placement and scaling."
---

Gameye uses both **regions** and **locations** in placement decisions, but they represent different levels.

- **Region**: a high-level geography or routing domain (for example, `eu-west`).
- **Location**: a concrete deployment target within that region (for example, a specific provider/site).

## How They Work Together

Matchmaking or orchestration logic typically selects a region first based on player proximity and capacity policy. Gameye then chooses an available location within that region to satisfy the launch request.

This model lets you balance latency, availability, and provider diversity without changing gameplay logic.
